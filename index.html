Meet Jalil

A person first.
An operator second.

Jalil Lewis grew up in Park Slope, Brooklyn — a neighborhood that taught him early that community is not just a backdrop, it is something you actively build. From the beginning, he was the kind of person people gravitated toward. Warm, perceptive, and genuinely curious about others. That quality was never something he cultivated for professional reasons. It was simply who he was. And it turned out to be exactly what healthcare needed.

What drives him most is taking something broken and making it work. Small business rehabilitation, protocol creation, turning operations from the red to the black to the green — these are not just professional interests. They are a calling. He believes deeply that the environment a team works in directly shapes the results they produce, and that the most meaningful business wins happen when the people inside the building actually feel supported.

His philosophy is rooted in a simple but powerful belief: every person who walks through a healthcare door is already carrying something. Maybe it is fear. Maybe it is a bill they cannot figure out. Maybe it is a diagnosis they do not fully understand yet. His job has always been to meet them there and make at least one part of that easier. He is committed to value-based care — the idea that healthcare should be measured not just by what is done, but by how it changes someone's life.

It is also worth saying plainly: everything Jalil has built — the protocols, the training systems, the pro bono cases, the practice growth — was accomplished while he was a full-time college student. Not after graduation. During. Every metric on this page was earned in parallel with coursework, deadlines, and everything else that comes with being a student. That context does not make the numbers bigger. It makes them mean something different.

With Annie (Youbin) Chung — the person who trained Jalil from day one at Lumia Dental.

The Philosophy

Business excellence
and human compassion
are not opposites.

The healthcare system is broken in ways that hurt real people every day. Patients walk in already overwhelmed — by insurance confusion, by financial stress, by procedures they do not fully understand. The system creates distance when it should be creating clarity.

Jalil's mission is to fix that from the inside. Not by overhauling the system overnight, but by making every single interaction clearer, warmer, and more manageable. When the systems work — when the billing flows, the scheduling holds, the training is solid, and the staff knows exactly what they are doing — patients feel it. They relax a little. They trust more. They heal better.

That is the standard he holds himself to: when the business runs well, the humanity of healthcare has room to show up. The two are not in tension. They are the same goal expressed in different ways.

Case Study

Lumia Dental, founded by Dr. Michelle Han, is a high-performance dental practice in Brooklyn. When Jalil joined under the mentorship of Dr. Raven Drummond, the practice had strong clinical talent but needed operational architecture to match. He built it.

Starting from scratch, Jalil designed and implemented the treatment coordination protocols that would define how the practice operated. He trained incoming staff, standardized patient communication, and systematized the financial workflow from consultation through case acceptance. The results compounded quickly.

$597K+ Protocol Revenue (through Q4 2025)
7 → 14 Operatories in 3 months
$6.8K–$8.9K Avg. case fee

The practice grew from 7 to 14 operatories between November 2023 and early 2024. Throughout that expansion, Jalil trained every incoming staff member — not just on systems, but on the standard of patient care that Dr. Drummond had set. The protocols held. The numbers reflected it.

What made the difference was not just operational rigor. It was the combination of genuine care for patients, a well-trained team, and systems that left no room for guesswork. When all three existed at once, the practice did not just grow. It transformed.

MedRite Urgent Care

Lab Technician · Clinical Support · Reception

Brooklyn, NY

The MedRite team — where Jalil first learned healthcare from the inside out.

MedRite Urgent Care gave Jalil something most people never get: a full cross-section of how healthcare actually runs. He did not come in as a specialist. He came in willing to learn, and he earned expanded responsibility at every stage.

He began as a lab technician, entering and managing patient results with precision and accountability. From there he moved into a hands-on clinical support role — drawing blood, working directly with patients, and developing the kind of composure that comes from being in the room when someone is scared or in pain. He then transitioned into reception, where he became the first point of contact for patients walking through the door.

That progression — clinical to patient-facing to administrative — gave him a perspective most healthcare operators never develop. He has been on every side of the desk. He knows what it feels like to draw someone's blood, to process their results, and then to explain those results to a worried family member at the front counter. That full-circle understanding is foundational to everything he does.

The Foundation

The mentor who set the standard.

Dr. Raven Drummond
Associate Endodontist, Lumia Dental

Two people from Brooklyn. One had built an extraordinary career through years of rigorous education, clinical training, and an uncompromising standard of care. The other was a college student with natural instincts for people and business, and a hunger to build something meaningful. Dr. Drummond saw that. And she gave Jalil the opportunity to prove it.

Dr. Raven Drummond is a native New Yorker, born and raised in Brooklyn. She earned her B.S. in Neuroscience and Behavioral Biology from Emory University and her Doctorate of Dental Surgery from Howard University. After graduating, she returned to New York to complete a general practice residency at Harlem Hospital, practiced as a general dentist across the city, and then pursued her postdoctoral degree in endodontics from Rutgers School of Dental Medicine. She is now an Associate Endodontist at Lumia Dental in Brooklyn, a practice founded by Dr. Michelle Han.

Annie Chung — known also as Youbin Chung — was the head treatment coordinator at Lumia Dental when Jalil started, and she was the person who trained him on the ground level from day one. She taught him the systems, the standards, and the rhythm of a high-performing practice. The knowledge he built on top of that foundation was only possible because of what she laid down first.

What Dr. Drummond gave Jalil was more than a job. She gave him a standard. She showed him what it looked like when clinical excellence and genuine patient care existed in the same room at the same time. Everything he built at Lumia — the protocols, the staff training, the practice growth, the pro bono cases — was built on the foundation she created. The mentorship shaped not just his career, but his entire philosophy around what healthcare should be and what it can do for people when it is working the way it should.

Rooted in Community

Where it started: Sunset Park

Growing up in Park Slope, Jalil understood early that community is not just where you live — it is what you do for the people around you. In high school he volunteered consistently with the Salvation Army GAINS Program in Sunset Park, Brooklyn. GAINS is an after-school youth development program serving children in one of Brooklyn's most underserved neighborhoods. He showed up, reliably, and gave his time to young people who needed someone dependable in their corner. That experience did not leave him. It became part of how he measures what success actually means.

The case that became a north star

A woman came to Lumia Dental referred by Providence House — a Brooklyn organization with over 40 years of experience helping women rebuild their lives after incarceration, domestic violence, and housing instability. She had survived things most people will never face. She had been through the justice system and was working, every single day, to build something new. When she walked in, what she wanted more than anything else was simple.

She wanted to be able to smile again.

Jalil coordinated a full pro bono treatment plan valued at $30,000. He managed the case from start to finish — navigating the clinical planning, the administrative coordination, and the deeply human side of sitting across from someone who needed to believe that people were genuinely in her corner. The work got done. She completed the full treatment program. She went on to take cooking classes and built a life she could be proud of.

That case is not a footnote. It is a north star. It is proof that when business systems run efficiently enough, they create space for generosity. That when you care about people, the work means something far beyond the numbers. Jalil's business acumen and his compassion are not two separate things. They are the same thing, expressed in different ways.
